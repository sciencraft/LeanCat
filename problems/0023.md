Theorem: Let $A,B\in\mathcal{R}\mathrm{ing}$ and let $\phi:A\to B$ be a morphism in $\mathcal{R}\mathrm{ing}$.
    It induces a functor $\phi_*:{}_B\mathcal{A}\mathrm{b}\to {}_A\mathcal{A}\mathrm{b}$, $(N,l_N)\mapsto (N,l_N\circ (\phi\otimes \mathrm{id}))$.
    Then the functor $\phi_*$ admits a left adjoint $\phi^*:=B\otimes_A -:{}_A\mathcal{A}\mathrm{b}\to {}_B\mathcal{A}\mathrm{b}$ and a right adjoint $\phi^!:=\hom_A(B,-):{}_A\mathcal{A}\mathrm{b}\to {}_B\mathcal{A}\mathrm{b}$.\nomenclature{${}_R\mathcal{A}\mathrm{b}$}{the category of left $R$-modules}
